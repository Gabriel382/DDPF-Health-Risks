Chandigarh: Flu symptoms? To be under watch for 28 days | Chandigarh News - Times of India 
 CHANDIGARH: The UT health department would keep a track on a person showing respiratory illness symptoms with a travel history to China or countries where the coronavirus is spreading for 28 days. This is in adherence to the guidelines of the ministry of health and family welfare. The Integrated Disease Surveillance Programme, Chandigarh, has started a helpline for queries on the virus.“All those coming from China are being thermally scanned at airports. However, those with mild symptoms will be given a list from the airports to be monitored,” said Dr Upendrajeet Singh Gill, state surveillance officer, Chandigarh. “These patients will be tracked by a team of doctors. Depending on the severity and symptoms, the surveillance will be either home-based or under hospital conditions. It does not mean than anyone traveling to China has coronavirus. It can be any other flu or virus,” Gill said.Though the doctors are not comfortable to talk about the virus for fear of creating panic, they said taking preventive measures were pertinent to stop transmission. “At this time of the season, it can be any other flu. Only those with travel history to China and with symptoms, must report to the nearby hospital. The Union ministry of health and family welfare has revised some of the treatment guidelines. The same shall be circulated soon,” said Prof Sanjay Jain, internal medicine department, PGI.The blood sample will be sent to the National Institute of Virology in Pune. “On high priority they have been directed to send the report the same day of receipt,” said Dr Gill.Presently, only travellers are being suspected with symptoms. “We have advised the doctors in the PGI to avoid attending any meetings in such countries,” said Prof Jagat Ram, director, PGI.Box:What doctors adviseIf you feel sick within a span of a month after return from China:Immediately call UT health department helpline 9779558282Maintain effective self-isolation at home and with othersObserve good personal hygiene.
