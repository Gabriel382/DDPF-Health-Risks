Gold futures settle lower, pulling back from a more than 6-year high 
 Gold futures settled lower on Tuesday, easing back from the more than six-year high they scored a day earlier on the back of concerns surrounding China's coronavirus. U.S. benchmark stock indexes climbed, easing haven demand for the metal. February gold GCG20, -0.45% fell $7.60, or 0.5%, to settle at $1,569.80 an ounce. Prices settled at $1,577.40 on Monday, the highest most-active contract finish since April 2013.
