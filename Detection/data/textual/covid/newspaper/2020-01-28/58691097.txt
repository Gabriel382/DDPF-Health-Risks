Tokyo stocks open lower amid continued coronavirus fears 
 TOKYO (Kyodo) -- Tokyo stocks opened lower Tuesday, tracking a global market downturn amid continued concerns about a deadly new coronavirus first identified in China.

In the first 15 minutes of trading, the 225-issue Nikkei Stock Average fell 213.31 points, or 0.91 percent, from Monday to 23,130.20. The broader Topix index of all First Section issues on the Tokyo Stock Exchange was down 16.80 points, or 0.99 percent, at 1,685.77.

Decliners were led by oil and coal product, mining and marine transportation issues.

In the currency market, the U.S. dollar remained weak against the yen as traders sought safe-haven assets.

At 9 a.m., the dollar fetched 108.93-94 yen, compared with 108.87-97 yen in New York and 109.05-07 yen in Tokyo at 5 p.m. Monday.

The euro was quoted at $1.1019-1020 and 120.03-04 yen against $1.1014-1024 and 119.93-120.03 yen in New York, and $1.1034-1035 and 120.33-37 yen in Tokyo late Monday afternoon.
