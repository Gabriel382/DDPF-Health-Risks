Economists counting the cost as coronavirus spreads panic 
 While health authorities worldwide have been battling to contain the spread of the deadly coronavirus, economists have been prescribing a dose of perspective to hypochondriac investors.
Fears about the economic impact of the respiratory infection are spreading fast in the markets, but economists say that any damage is likely to be short-lived. They acknowledge that people have died from the coronavirus and that there are likely to be more victims in the near term, but they also point to the Sars epidemic of 2002-03, a severe acute respiratory syndrome that infected more than 8,000 people worldwide, killing 774.
