AMD stock slides as outlook falls below Wall Street view 
 Advanced Micro Devices Inc. shares declined in the extended session Tuesday after the chip maker’s outlook fell below Wall Street estimates and data-center sales did not come in as well as expected.

AMD AMD, +2.58%  shares dropped 3% after hours, following a 2.6% rise in the regular session to close at $50.53.

The company reported fourth-quarter net income of $170 million, or 15 cents a share, compared with $38 million, or 4 cents a share, in the year-ago period. Adjusted earnings were 32 cents a share.

Revenue rose to $2.13 billion from $1.42 billion in the year-ago quarter.

Analysts surveyed by FactSet had forecast earnings of 31 cents on revenue of $2.11 billion.

In AMD’s largest segment, computing and graphics chips, the company reported sales of $1.66 billion, while analysts expected a 52% rise in sales to $1.5 billion, according to FactSet.

Sales of enterprise embedded and semi-custom segment, which include AMD’s Epyc line of data-center chips, came in at $465 million, while analysts had expected sales to rise 39% to $603.8 million.

AMD expects revenue of $1.75 billion to $1.85 billion for the first quarter, while analysts had forecast revenue of $1.86 billion.

Over the past 12 months, AMD shares have rallied 149%. In comparison, the S&P 500 index SPX, +1.01% has gained 24%, the tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite Index COMP, +1.43%   has grown 31%, and the PHLX Semiconductor Index SOX, +2.40% has increased 51%.

Of the 38 analysts who cover AMD, 15 have buy or overweight ratings, 20 have hold ratings and three have sell or underweight ratings, with an average price target of $43.30.
