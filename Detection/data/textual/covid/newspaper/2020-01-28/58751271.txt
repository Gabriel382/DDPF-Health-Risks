Chinese netizens vent anger on social media amid coronavirus outbreak 
 Chinese internet users rarely have the opportunity to vent their anger at local government officials on social media due to the country’s strict censorship but the coronavirus outbreak may have changed that – at least temporarily.Weibo, a Twitter-like microblogging site, and Tencent’s ubiquitous WeChat all purpose app, have emerged as channels for criticism directed at the government of Hubei, the Chinese province at the epicentre of the outbreak, with 1,771 new confirmed cases of the virus…
