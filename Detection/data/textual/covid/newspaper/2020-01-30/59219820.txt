Coronavirus looks like biggest threat to Apple’s remarkable turnaround 
 Over the past year, Tim Cook has performed a conjuring act of a kind rarely seen on the stock market. The boss of Apple has managed to double its share price, adding about three quarters of a trillion dollars to the value of the iPhone maker.
It is an extraordinary figure even by the grandiose standards of Silicon Valley. For $750 billion, you could snap up Facebook and still have $100 billion in change. Alternatively, you could buy London’s four most valuable public companies: Royal Dutch Shell, Unilever, HSBC and Astrazeneca.
