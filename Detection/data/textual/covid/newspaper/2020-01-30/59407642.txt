Jan 30 (Reuters) - Gold prices held firm on Thursday as concerns of a slowdown in global growth due to a virus outbreak in China weakened risk appetite among investors.

Spot gold  was up 0.1% at $1,577.69 per ounce by 10:07 a.m. EST (1507 GMT) after rising 0.7% in the last session.
