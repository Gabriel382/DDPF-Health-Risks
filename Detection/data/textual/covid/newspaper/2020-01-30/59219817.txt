Federal Reserve keeps US base rate unchanged 
 The chairman of the US Federal Reserve warned that the coronavirus posed a threat to the world economy as the central bank held its base rate steady last night.
Jerome Powell said the virus, which has killed at least 132 people and infected nearly 6,000 in China, was a “very serious issue” that was likely to affect global growth.
Federal Reserve policymakers voted unanimously to hold the base rate in a range of 1.5 per cent to 1.75 per cent, as expected. It has been at that level since the central bank cut rates in October last year in a third successive reduction, having raised rates four times in 2018.
