PRESS DIGEST - Bulgaria - Jan 30 
 SOFIA, Jan 30 (Reuters) - These are some of the main stories in Bulgarian newspapers on Thursday. Reuters has not verified these stories and does not vouch for their accuracy.

-- Two Bulgarian women who recently returned from China and Thailand have been put under observation in hospitals for coronavirus, Health Minister Kiril Ananiev said. (24 Chasa, Monitor, Trud)

-- Dozens of Bulgarians protested against the government in downtown Sofia, angered by its plans to introduce new software systems to control sales. The finance ministry has already said it would delay the new rules by six months. (Duma)

-- There is a political consensus in Bulgaria for eurozone entry, Finance Minister Vladislav Goranov said after meeting with parliamentary presented political factions. He reiterated Bulgaria plans to keep its lev currency peg to the euro at 1.95583 until euro adoption expected in 2023. (24 Chasa, Trud, Monitor)

TRUD - Extensive drought has hit on wheat sowings and the 2020 harvest is likely to be weaker, senior member of grain producers association said.
