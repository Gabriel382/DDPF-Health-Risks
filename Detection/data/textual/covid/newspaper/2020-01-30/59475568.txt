Still no novel coronavirus cases in Alberta despite WHO declaring emergency 
 EDMONTON -- 
	There are still no probable or confirmed cases of novel coronavirus in Alberta, health officials say after the World Health Organization declared an emergency.


	Still, Alberta Chief Medical Officer of Health Dr. Deena Hinshaw will provide an update at 4 p.m. on what the province is doing to prepare for coronavirus.


	China has reported more than 7,800 cases and 170 deaths caused by the respiratory illness. Eighteen other countries have reported cases.


	So far, three cases have been confirmed in Canada. Two people in Ontario and one person in B.C. have fallen ill with coronavirus.


	CTVNewsEdmonton.ca will livestream the Alberta Health Services news conference at 4 p.m.
