Virus-hit markets ‘could drop by 10%’ 
 The deadly virus outbreak spreading from China could wipe 10 per cent off the value of world stock markets, a broker warned yesterday as international businesses intensified efforts to scale back operations in the country.
Société Générale, the French bank, said a “further escalation of the coronavirus could result in an overall 10 per cent equity market correction” and recommended clients avoid taking on more risk until the outbreak stabilises.
Hong Kong’s stock market fell 2.4 per cent on its first trading session since closing for the lunar new year holiday, hitting a seven-week low, but global markets recouped losses for a second day after a sell-off earlier this week.
