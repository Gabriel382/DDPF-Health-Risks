Wall Street closes higher after late session comeback 
 (Reuters) - U.S. stocks stages a late session rebound to close higher on Thursday after comments from the World Health Organization (WHO) that declared the China coronavirus a global emergency, while earnings painted a mixed picture. 
The Dow Jones Industrial Average .DJI rose 124.08 points, or 0.43%, to 28,858.53, the SP 500 .SPX gained 10.19 points, or 0.31%, to 3,283.59 and the Nasdaq Composite .IXIC added 23.77 points, or 0.26%, to 9,298.93.
