CANADA STOCKS-TSX opens lower as oil prices tumble on coronavirus fears 
 Jan 30 (Reuters) - Canada’s main stock index fell broadly at the open on Thursday, with losses led by energy companies, as crude prices were hit by fears of an economic fallout from a fast spreading viral outbreak in China. 
* At 09:30 a.m. ET (14:30 GMT), the Toronto Stock Exchange’s SP/TSX composite index was down 79.87 points, or 0.46%, at 17,431.88. (Reporting by Susan Mathew in Bengaluru; Editing by Amy Caren Daniel)
