NEW DELHI: After India reported the first case of coronavirus from Kerala , China said it was paying close attention to the case and that it was going to jointly work with India to strengthen efforts to prevent and control the epidemic which originated in Wuhan , the capital of Hubei’s province. While Beijing was yet to approve India’s request to operate two aircraft to fly back Indian nationals, the Indian side was hoping evacuation will finally begin Friday evening."Indian nationals have been alerted about evacuation plans for Friday evening. It will materialise though only if Chinese authorities approve it," said a source here late on Thursday evening.The China embassy here expressed hope in a statement that the Indian government will show understanding and support for China’s efforts to deal with coronavirus. It said the virus was controllable and curable and that Wuhan was stocked with enough daily necessity supplies.The Indian government said Thursday that it had contacted more than 600 Indian nationals in the Hubei province to ascertain their willingness to be repatriated. MEA spokesperson Raveesh Kumar said India was hoping approval to evacuate Indian nationals who want to return will be granted very soon.“Our Mission in Beijing is working round-the-clock on the ground to work out the necessary logistics. Arrangements are being put in place for people to travel to the airport. We appreciate the support extended by the Government of China and other ministries in the government of India in putting this process together,’’ said the official.He also said that India had not put any restriction on movement of tourists from China to India.The Chinese embassy said here that it had maintained close communication with the Indian government, timely briefed Indian officials on the development of the epidemic and China’s efforts in epidemic prevention and control.``The Chinese government attaches great importance to safeguarding the safety and health of every foreign national in China, including the Indian people. We are willing to continue to maintain close communication with the Indian side, provide necessary assistance and convenience to ensure the health and safety of Indian citizens in China, and address their legitimate concerns timely,’’ it said.It further said that China believed the international community would assess the epidemic situation in an objective, just, calm and reasonable way, and will continue to strengthen international cooperation to jointly tackle the epidemic and safeguard regional and global public health security.“The epidemic is generally preventable, controllable and curable. At present, there are sufficient supplies of daily necessities in Wuhan, and the commodity prices are also generally stable. The Chinese government has mobilised all the national resources to ensure the supply of medical equipment and living materials in Wuhan. We have full capability, confidence and resources to win the battle against the epidemic at an early date,’’ it added.The Chinese claimed they had acted with openness, transparency and “a high sense of responsibility’’ and that the Chinese government had shared information of the epidemic with WHO and “relevant nations and regions’’ in a timely manner.
