Philippines confirms first case of new coronavirus: health minister 
 A man wearing a mask sits in a convenience store in Paranaque, Metro Manila, Philippines, January 30, 2020. REUTERS/Eloisa Lopez


MANILA (Reuters) - Philippine health officials have confirmed the first case of the new coronavirus in the country.     
A 38-year-old Chinese woman, who arrived in the country from Wuhan, China, on Jan. 21, tested positive for the novel coronavirus, Health Secretary Francisco Duque told a news conference.  
Duque said the patient, confined in a government hospital, is currently asymptomatic.
