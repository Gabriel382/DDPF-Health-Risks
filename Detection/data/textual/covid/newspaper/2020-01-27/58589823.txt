Coronavirus China: India to prepare for evacuation of citizens from coronavirus-hit Chinese city | India News - Times of India 
 NEW DELHI: The government on Monday decided to prepare for possible evacuation of Indian nationals from Wuhan city in Coronavirus-hit Chinese province of Hubei The decision was taken at a high-level meeting chaired by cabinet secretary Rajiv Gauba which carried out a detailed review of the situation arising out of outbreak of Coronavirus in China.Officials said the external affairs ministry will request to Chinese authorities for evacuation of Indians from Wuhan.The death toll from a coronavirus outbreak in China rose to 80 on Monday.The officials said the shipping ministry will initiate screening of people at international ports having traffic from China.As on January 26, 29,707 passengers from 137 flights have been screened for novel coronovirus (nCoV) .However, no case has so far been detected in the country.Thermal screening is being done at seven designated airports - New Delhi, Kolkata, Mumbai, Chennai, Bengaluru, Hyderabad and Kochi.The Centre has also stepped up vigil in areas bordering Nepal in view of a confirmed case of novel coronavirus detected there.States have informed that they have made all necessary arrangements for screening of people transiting across the border.Over 100 people have been kept under observation in Kerala and Maharashtra following screening for a possible exposure to the virus.Novel coronavirus (nCoV) is a large family of viruses that causes illnesses ranging from common cold to acute respiratory syndromes, but the virus that has so far killed 81 people and affected 2,744 in China is a novel strain and not seen before.It has emerged from a seafood and animal market in China's Wuhan, and is suspected to have spread to as far as the United States. According to the World Health Organisation, the common symptoms of the novel coronavirus strain include respiratory symptoms such as fever, cough, shortness of breath and breathing difficulties.
