Sensex dives 458 points on fears of coronavirus spread; Nifty ends at 12,119 - Times of India 
 NEW DELHI: Equity indices finished in red on Monday following its Asian peers amid fears about the economic impact of China's spreading virus outbreak. The deadly coronavirus has so far claimed more than 80 lives in China.The benchmark BSE sensex cracked dived 458 points or 1.10 per cent to close at 41,155; while the broader NSE Nifty setlled 129 points or 1.06 per cent lower at 12,119.Top laggards in the 30-share BSE pack include Tata Steel, IndusInd Bank, HDFC twins (HDFC and HDFC Bank), SBI and Bharti Airtel with their shares falling as much as 4.66 per cent. On NSE, except for Nifty Pharma, all other sub-indices witnessed losses with Nifty Metal and PSU Bank sliding as much as 3.09 per cent."It is a global scare. It has a global economic impact and impacts commodity prices," Deepak Jasani, head of retail research at HDFC Securities Ltd in Mumbai, told news agency Reuters as the virus fears spooked markets.This is China's peak travel season but it has halted outbound tours and some of its other cities even have stopped public transport due to the virus outbreak."Foreign investors may want to reduce positions across markets," Jasani added.Analysts also expect stock indices to remain volatile till the Union Budget 2020, which may include government measures to revive economic growth that has slipped to a more than six-year low. Budget will be presented on February 1.Meanwhile, on a net basis, foreign institutional investors (FIIs) bought equities worth Rs 659.11 crore, while domestic institutional investors (DIIs) purchased shares worth Rs 417.96 crore on Friday, data available with stock exchanges showed.(With agency inputs)
