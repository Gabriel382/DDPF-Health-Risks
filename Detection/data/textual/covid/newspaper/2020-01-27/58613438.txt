Global markets catch a cold as fears grow over coronavirus 
 The FTSE 100 suffered its worst day since last October as growing concern about the impact of coronavirus infected world stock markets.
Wall Street slumped by its most in nearly four months as traders bet that the American Federal Reserve would bring forward an expected interest rate cut.
British health chiefs said that coronavirus, a deadly pneumonia-like infection that has spread across the world from Wuhan in China, was likely to already be in the country, as the US confirmed its fifth case.
There are 2,744 confirmed infections but scientists believe that as many as 100,000 people worldwide are infected. At least 80 people have died from the virus.
