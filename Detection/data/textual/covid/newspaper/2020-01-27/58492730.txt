Romania - Factors to watch on Jan. 27 
 BUCHAREST, Jan 27 (Reuters) - Here are news stories, press reports and events to watch which may affect Romanian financial markets on Monday.

Debt managers plan to sell 500 million lei ($116.11 million)worth of 2023 treasury bonds at tender.

The finance ministry sold a planned 300 million lei ($69.67 million) worth of 2034 treasury bonds on Thursday at an average accepted yield of 4.78%, central bank data showed.

It last sold 2034 paper in December at 5.07%.

Central European currencies gained on Friday, as better than expected PMI data from the euro zone helped restore risk appetite that had taken a hit in recent days from the outbreak of coronavirus in China.
