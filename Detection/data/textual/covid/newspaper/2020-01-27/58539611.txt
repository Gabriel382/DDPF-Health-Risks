Investors ditch equities as fears grow over coronavirus 
 Concerns about the possible economic impact of the coronavirus on China has pushed down oil prices and boosted gold as investors sell shares to buy safe-haven assets.
 Brent crude fell below $60 a barrel after the flu-like virus killed 81 people and infected more than 2,700 in China and 2,000 further afield.
 The FTSE 100 fell 1.7 per cent, or 130 points, in early trading to 7,455. In Tokyo the Nikkei average slid 2 per cent, its biggest one-day fall in five months. Many markets in Asia, including China and Hong Kong, are closed for the Chinese New Year but a Tokyo-listed proxy for Chinese stocks, the ChinaAMC CSI 300 index ETF, fell 2.2 per cent.
