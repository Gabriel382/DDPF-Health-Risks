Matildas' Olympic qualifiers moved due to Coronavirus 
 Olympic qualifying games for the women's soccer tournament have been moved from Wuhan to another Chinese city due to a deadly virus outbreak.
The Asian Football Confederation confirmed on Wednesday the round-robin group – hosts China, Australia, Taiwan and Thailand – will be played in Nanjing on the originally scheduled dates next month.
Ante Milicic and Sam Kerr's Matildas will now play qualifying in Nanjing.Credit:Getty Images
Chinese authorities have shut down transport out of the city of 11 million people during Lunar New Year, as the death count from the flu-like illness rises to 17.
The Asian soccer body's decision was announced hours before a World Health Organisation meeting in Geneva, which said it would wait until Friday before deciding whether to declare the outbreak a global crisis.
