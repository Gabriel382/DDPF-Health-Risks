Stocks start mostly lower but Amazon a big early gainer 
 Stocks slide at the open, weighed by lingering worries over the coronavirus outbreak and perhaps end-of-the-month profit-taking and rebalancing efforts; Dow , S&P , Nasdaq .

The Dow lags following disappointing earnings results and/or guidance from Caterpillar ( ), Exxon ( ) and Visa ( ), while Amazon's ( ) stellar earnings report makes the Nasdaq an early outperformer.

European bourses trade lower, with U.K.'s FTSE and Germany's DAX and France's CAC both ; in Asia, Japan's Nikkei and Hong Kong's Hang Seng , while China's Shanghai Composite remains closed.

In the U.S., a look at the S&P 500 sectors shows consumer discretionary ( ) far ahead of the other groups on the back of strength in Amazon, while energy ( ), materials ( ) and financials ( ) trade noticeably lower.

U.S. Treasury prices tick up slightly, pushing both the two-year and 10-year yields a basis point lower to 1.38% and 1.55%, respectively; U.S. Dollar Index to 97.69.
