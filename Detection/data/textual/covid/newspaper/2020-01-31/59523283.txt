Wuhan coronavirus may hit North American airlines harder than SARS as global emergency grows 
 The coronavirus outbreak in Wuhan, China could cost North American airlines far more money than a previous deadly outbreak in China did in 2003, a top airline research firm says.

Citing International Air Transport Association data, research firm IBA Group said in a report Thursday that the 2003 SARS (severe acute respiratory syndrome) coronavirus outbreak cost Asia Pacific airlines about $6 billion in lost revenue.  North American airlines lost another $1 billion 17 years ago, IBA said.
