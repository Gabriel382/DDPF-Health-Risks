Energy stocks suffer broad selloff after Exxon Mobil, Chevron results and oil drops again 
 Energy stocks were suffering a broad selloff Friday, as disappointing earnings reports from the industry leaders and further declines in oil prices took a toll. The SPDR Energy Select Sector ETF XLE, -2.84% dropped 2.9% toward a four-year low, with 27 of 28 components losing ground. Among the more-active components, shares of Exxon Mobil Corp. XOM, -3.79% slumped 3.6% and Chevron Corp. CVX, -3.53% shed 3.5% after both companies reported earnings that missed expectations, according to FactSet. Meanwhile, crude oil futures CL00, -1.13% slumped 0.9% toward a near 6-month low amid demand concerns as the coronavirus spreads. Crude futures have not tumbled 15.5% this month. Natural gas futures NG00, +0.77% bounced 1.2%, after closing at a four-year low on Thursday. The energy ETF has now lost 7.6% over the past three months, while the Dow Jones Industrial Average DJIA, -1.26% has advanced 5.4%.
