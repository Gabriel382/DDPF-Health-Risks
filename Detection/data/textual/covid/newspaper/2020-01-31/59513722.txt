Japan's ANA may consider halting China flights - Jiji citing CEO 
 TOKYO (Reuters) - Japan’s top airline, All Nippon Airways, may need to consider suspending flights bound for China, Jiji news agency cited the company’s chief executive, Yuji Hirako, as saying on Friday. 
The report comes as pilots and flight attendants are demanding airlines to stop flights to China as health officials declare a global emergency over the rapidly spreading coronavirus.
