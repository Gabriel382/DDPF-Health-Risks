We’re ready for any coronavirus victims, say health chiefs 
 There is a “moderate” risk of a coronavirus outbreak in Ireland, but health experts have said that they are prepared to deal with any cases.
While there have been no confirmed cases of the virus in Ireland, a number of tests have been carried out on concerned patients.
Speaking at an HSE briefing in Dublin yesterday, Dr John Cuddihy, director of the Health Protection Surveillance Centre, advised anyone who had developed symptoms and had travelled to the centre of the coronavirus outbreak in China to ring their GP.
