Asia stocks trade higher as investors await official Chinese manufacturing data 
 Asia markets traded higher on Friday morning as investors await the release of Chinese official manufacturing data for January amid coronavirus fears.

The World Health Organization has declared a global health emergency over the the coronavirus outbreak that has killed at least 200 in China.

In Japan, the Nikkei 225 gained 0.88% as shares of index heavyweights Softbank Group and Fanuc rose more than 1% each. The Topix index also added 0.73%. South Korea's Kospi also advanced 0.54%.

Shares in Australia rose in morning trade, with the S&P/ASX 200 up 0.45%.

The release of China's official Purchasing Managers' Index for January is set to be around 9:00 a.m. HK/SIN, amid Beijing's efforts to contain an ongoing virus outbreak in the country that has sent jitters across global markets in recent days.
