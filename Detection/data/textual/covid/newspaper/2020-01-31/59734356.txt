Qantas plane on its way to Wuhan to rescue hundreds of Australians 
 A Qantas plane is en route to Wuhan to rescue some of the 600 Australians trapped in the Chinese city plagued by coronavirus.

The Qantas 747 will touchdown in the epicentre of deadly virus via Hong Kong, before transporting hundreds of trapped Australians to Christmas Island.

The flight is expected to land in Darwin at 2.30am on Monday and evacuees will then be moved to the detention centre.
