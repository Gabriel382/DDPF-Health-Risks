Wuhan virus: Britain reports first two cases of coronavirus 
 LONDON (AFP) - Health officials in Britain said on Friday (Jan 31) that two people had tested positive for the novel coronavirus, the first confirmed cases in the country since the deadly outbreak began in China.

"We can confirm that two patients in England, who are members of the same family, have tested positive for coronavirus," said Chris Whitty, Chief Medical Officer for England, adding that they were getting "specialist care".
