US stocks fall 570 points on worries about coronavirus outbreak 
 NEW YORK -- U.S. stocks lost ground on Friday as investors grew concerned about the the coronavirus outbreak's impact on the global economy, sending the benchmarkÂ Dow Jones Industrial Average down 570 points at one point in afternoon trading.
The selling affected a wide range of stocks as risk aversion mode took hold on Wall Street.
The virus is spreading rapidly, with the U.K. confirming its first case Friday. Some U.S. companies are now banning employees from visiting China, while the State Department issued a travel advisory on Thursday urging Americans to not travel to mainland China.
Delta Air Lines announced Friday that it will suspend all flights to and from China between Feb. 6 and the end of April, with American Airlines and United Airlines taking similar steps.
Investor sentiment is cooling amid growing concerns over the outbreak's impact on the global economy. The inverted yield curve, considered a precursor to a recession, is widening in the U.S. as yields on short-term government bonds soar above the benchmark 10-year yields.
