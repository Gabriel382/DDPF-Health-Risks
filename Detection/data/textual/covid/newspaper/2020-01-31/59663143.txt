S&P 500 opens lower on virus concerns, Amazon supports Nasdaq 
 FILE PHOTO: Trader Michael Urkonis works on the floor of the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) in New York, U.S., January 28, 2020. REUTERS/Bryan R Smith/File Photo


(Reuters) - The SP 500 and the Dow Jones Industrial Average opened lower on Friday, hit by worries over the impact of the coronavirus epidemic on global growth, while the Nasdaq was supported by strong earnings from Amazon.com Inc.  
The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 46.40 points, or 0.16%, at the open to 28,813.04. The SP 500 opened lower by 1.33 points, or 0.04%, at 3,282.33. The Nasdaq Composite gained 25.39 points, or 0.27%, to 9,324.33 at the opening bell.
