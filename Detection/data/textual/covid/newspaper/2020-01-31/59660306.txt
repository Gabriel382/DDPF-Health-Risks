US STOCKS SNAPSHOT-S&P 500 opens lower on virus concerns, Amazon... 
 Jan 31 (Reuters) - The S&P 500 and the Dow Jones Industrial Average opened lower on Friday, hit by worries over the impact of the coronavirus epidemic on global growth, while the Nasdaq was supported by strong earnings from Amazon.com Inc.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 46.40 points, or 0.16%, at the open to 28,813.04. The S&P 500 opened lower by 1.33 points, or 0.04%, at 3,282.33. The Nasdaq Composite gained 25.39 points, or 0.27%, to 9,324.33 at the opening bell. (Reporting by Sruthi Shankar in Bengaluru; Editing by Anil D'Silva)
