Protest-hit Hong Kong fears virus threat 
 After months of damaging protests that had already tipped Hong Kong into recession, the territory is fighting a fresh crisis that is raising questions over its future as a global economic centre.
Hong Kong has declared the coronavirus outbreak an emergency and yesterday its authorities pulled up the drawbridge to the mainland.
Carrie Lam, the territory’s chief executive, restricted flights, trains and ferries from China, ordered increased inspections at border checkpoints and suspended visas for visitors to Hong Kong. “We have to start with the source,” she said.
