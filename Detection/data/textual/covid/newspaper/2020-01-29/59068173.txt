First coronavirus cases reported in Mideast 
 The first cases of coronavirus were confirmed in the United Arab Emirates, the Ministry of Health and Prevention announced Wednesday.

The cases were discovered in a four-member Chinese family, according to UAE health officials.

“All of them are in stable condition and contained according to the internally approved precautions,” the ministry said in the statement.

Officials did not say where the family was being treated or when they arrived.

The virus originated in Wuhan, China, and has spread across the globe. It has killed more than 130 people in China, according to Reuters.

Five cases have been confirmed in the U.S. as of Monday, according to the Centers for Disease Control. The cases are spread across four states: Washington, California, Arizona and Illinois.
