14 more quarantined, samples sent for coronavirus check | India News - Times of India 
 NEW DELHI: Fourteen more people were quarantined on Tuesday in hospitals and at home in Hyderabad, Mumbai, Bengaluru, Goa, Gurgaon, Faridabad, Nuh and Panipat for tests to check whether they had contracted the new strain of coronavirus (nCoV). Samples from most had been taken and sent to NIV, Pune for testing.Of the 14 patients, five had been quarantined at their homes in Haryana (two in Gurgaon and one each in Faridabad, Nuh and Panipat). Haryana DG of health services Dr S B Kamboj said two of the five had exhibited multiple symptoms. The Centre, meanwhile, announced that four labs at Alleppey, Bengaluru, Hyderabad and Mumbai — would begin testing samples for nCoV. Meanwhile, five more people from Hyderabad were put in isolation on Tuesday.In Bengaluru, two more people suspected of exposure to nCoV were admitted to Rajiv Gandhi Institute of Chest Diseases on Tuesday. Both had travelled to China and had complained of symptoms of the virus. In Mumbai, a resident with a travel history to Wuhan was quarantined on Tuesday. Eight patients — five in Mumbai, two in Pune and one in Nanded — are in isolation wards across hospitals in Maharashtra.
