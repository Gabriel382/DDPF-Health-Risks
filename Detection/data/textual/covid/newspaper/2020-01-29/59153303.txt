KLM says it will scrap some flights to China due to the coronavirus outbreak 
 FILE PHOTO: A KLM commercial passenger jet takes off in Blagnac near Toulouse, France, May 29, 2019. REUTERS/Regis Duvignau/File Photo


AMSTERDAM (Reuters) - KLM, the Dutch arm of Air France KLM (AIRF.PA), will scrap some flights to China due to the coronavirus outbreak in the North Asian country, it said late on Wednesday.  
Starting on Friday, direct flights to Chengdu and Hangzhou will be suspended, while the number flights to Shanghai will be reduced from 11 to 7 per week.  
Service to Beijing has not been affected, KLM said in a statement.
