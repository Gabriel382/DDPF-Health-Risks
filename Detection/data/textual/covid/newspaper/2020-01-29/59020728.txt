Hong Kong gold market makes best lunar new year debut since 2016 
 The Hong Kong gold market rose on the first trading day of the Year of the Rat on Wednesday, rising to its best lunar year debut since 2016, as investors flocked to the safe haven asset amid concerns over the Wuhan coronavirus outbreak.The gold market closed at HK$14,353 (US$1,846) a tael, or 37.9 grams, an increase of HK$125 or 0.9 per cent over its close on Friday, the last trading day of the Year of the Pig.
