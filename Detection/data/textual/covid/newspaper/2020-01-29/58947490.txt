Government seeks China help on evacuating Indians | India News - Times of India 
 NEW DELHI: As China struggles to contain the rapidly spreading novel coronavirus , India has made a formal request to Beijing for facilitating evacuation of Indians stuck in Wuhan , the epicentre of the outbreak.The government on Tuesday said the Indian embassy in Beijing is in contact with the Indian nationals, many of them medical students, and the civil aviation ministry will make necessary arrangements for evacuation after receiving approval from the Chinese authorities.“Indian embassy is in constant touch with the Chinese government. To evacuate students and other Indians from Wuhan, we are planning to send a plane there. Our efforts are on to bring them back. It will take some days. I urge people to trust the government on this,” Jaishankar said . “No Indian student has been found to be affected by this virus. Parents need not worry about their children,” he said.The DGCA on Tuesday gave its nod to the special flight by Air India on Mumbai-Wuhan-Delhi route to evacuate the Indians. A 423-seater AI Boeing 747 has been kept on standby to fly as soon as the clearance comes from China, sources said. The health ministry said, “On evacuation, these individuals will be kept in quarantine for 14 days.”
