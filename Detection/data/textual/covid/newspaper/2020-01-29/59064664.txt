New coronavirus cases in China surpass SARS epidemic as infections grow abroad 
 What you need to know about coronavirus

Follow our updates: More than 6,000 cases have been confirmed in China, a number that surpasses those stricken by the 2002-2003 SARS epidemic.

Mapping the spread of the new coronavirus: The United States, Germany, Sri Lanka, France, Thailand, Japan, Taiwan, Nepal, Hong Kong, Singapore, Vietnam, Macao and South Korea have all confirmed cases of the infection.

U.S. health officials confirmed three new U.S. cases, bringing the total to five. The CDC raised its travel warning to a level 3, urging U.S. citizens to avoid all nonessential travel to China.

In Wuhan, the Chinese city of 11 million people where the coronavirus outbreak began last month, train stations, ferries, buses and the airport all have shut down. Travel bans were extended in central China to put more than 50 million people effectively on local lockdowns. Beijing has shut down schools indefinitely.

What is coronavirus and how does it spread? Coronaviruses are a large family of viruses whose effects range from causing the common cold to triggering much more serious diseases, such as severe acute respiratory syndrome, or SARS.
