Stock futures edge higher as earnings overshadow coronavirus worries 
 Stock-index futures pointed to a slightly higher start for Wall Street on Wednesday, with corporate earnings overshadowing concerns over China’s coronavirus outbreak.

Futures on the Dow Jones Industrial Average YMH20, +0.50%  rose 121 points, or 0.4%, to 28,830, while S&P 500 futures ESH20, +0.35%  gained 11.45 points, or 0.3%, to trade at 3,289.75. Nasdaq-100 futures NQH20, +0.51%  were up 40.75 points, or 0.5%, at 9,147.75.

The Dow DJIA, +0.66%  on Tuesday rose 187.05 points, or 0.7%, to end at 28,722.85, snapping a five-day losing streak and taking back a chunk of the previous session’s decline, which was the steepest one-day fall since October. The S&P 500 SPX, +1.01%, which has also suffered its biggest one-day drop since October on Monday, rose 32.61 points, or 1%, to finish at 3,276.24. The Nasdaq Composite COMP, +1.43%  closed at 9,269.68, up 130.37 points, or 1.4%.

Stocks were on track to build on Tuesday’s recovery, with investors playing down the spread of the coronavirus, which has now infected at least 5,327 people in China, as the death toll in the country rose to 132.

The United Arab Emirates on Wednesday confirmed the first Mideast cases of the virus. Cases have also been reported in Taiwan, Germany, Vietnam and Japan included patients who haven’t been to China, according to the New York Times. No deaths outside of China have been reported.

“Markets are showing small gains, which suggests that dealers aren’t overly confident the situation will just drop off the radar anytime soon. Neither are they running for the hills, so perhaps they are waiting to find out the financial cost of the crisis,” said David Madden, market analyst at CMC, in a note.

Shares of Apple Inc. AAPL, +2.83% were up in premarket trade after delivering strong results after Tuesday’s closing bell, while investors prepared to wade through what could be the busiest single day of earnings season. Nearly 10% of the S&P 500 were scheduled to report Wednesday, including four members of the blue-chip Dow Jones Industrial Average.

The Federal Reserve is fully expected to leave rates unchanged when it concludes a two-day policy meeting Wednesday afternoon, but the central bank and its chairman, Jerome Powell, might face a challenge in reassuring market participants as it makes technical adjustments.

“We fully expect Chair Powell to repeat the December mantra, namely, the current level of interest rates is ‘appropriate’ and that a ‘material’ change to the outlook would be required to persuade the Fed to ease again,” said Ian Shepherdson, chief economist at Pantheon Macroeconomics, in a note. “That bar has not been met.”

Investors will also be watching for data on advance trade in goods for December, due at 8:30 a.m. Eastern Time, while a reading on December pending home sales is set for 10 a.m.

Boeing Co. BA, -0.01%  reported a surprise loss for the fourth quarter and revenue that declined below analyst expectations amid the company’s struggles linked to the extended grounding of its 737 Max airplanes. The Dow component swung to a loss of $2.33 per share, excluding non-recurring items, versus the FactSet consensus of $1.32 earnings per share. Revenue, meanwhile fell 37% to $17.91 billion, below the $21.70 billion consensus. Shares nevertheless rose 2.1% in premarket trade, though they have lost 9.3% during the past 3 months, versus a 6.1% rise for the Dow.

Shares of chip-maker Advanced Micro Devices Inc. AMD, +2.58%  were down 4.5% ahead of the bell. AMD late Tuesday offered an outlook that was weaker than Wall Street analysts had expected, while data-center sales disappointed.

Shares of online auction site eBay Inc. EBAY, +2.06%  were down 4.7% in premarket action. The company late Tuesday reported fourth-quarter earnings that beat the consensus forecast, but offered weak first-quarter revenue guidance.

Coffee-chain Starbucks Corp. SBUX, -0.15%  late Tuesday topped forecasts with its fiscal first-quarter earnings and called its holiday season “one of the strongest” in its history. But it also warned that China’s coronavirus outbreak was likely to hit its bottom line this year. Shares were 0.1% lower ahead of the bell.

Shares of Dow Inc. DOW, -0.04%  gained 3.3% in premarket action after the materials sciences company reported adjusted profit and sales that fell less than expected.

Victoria Secret parent L Brands Inc. LB, +1.88%  is exploring strategic alternatives for the lingerie brand, including a full or partial sale, according to a report in the Wall Street Journal. L Brands’ stock rose 10.9% in premarket trade.

Shares of Xilinx Inc. XLNX, +1.66%  were down more than 7% in premarket trade after it reported fourth-quarter guidance late Tuesday that came in below consensus estimates but also reported stronger-than-expected third-quarter sales.

Shares of General Electric Co. GE, +2.53%   rose nearly 8% in premarket trading after the industrial conglomerate reported fourth-quarter profit and revenue that topped expectations, while its earning outlook was below forecasts.

McDonald’s Corp. MCD, +0.50%  on Wednesday reported fourth-quarter earnings and revenue that beat expectations. Shares were down 1.2% in premarket trade.

Shares of AT&T Inc. T, +0.86% T, +0.86% were off slightly in premarket trade after the media and telecommunications giant reported better-than-expected fourth-quarter earnings but fell short of forecasts on revenue.

In the bond market, the yield on the 10-year U.S. Treasury note TMUBMUSD10Y, -1.72%  fell one basis point to 1.631%.

Crude oil prices were edging higher Wednesday, with the price of a barrel of West Texas Intermediate crude CLH20, +0.92%  rising 35 cents, or 0.7% to $53.83. In precious metals, GCG20, -0.03%  rose $1.40, or 0.1%, to $1571.10 an ounce.

The U.S. dollar edged 0.1% higher against a basket of its rival currencies, according to the ICE U.S. Dollar index DXY, +0.14%  .

European stocks were ticking mostly higher, as the Stoxx Europe 600 SXXP, +0.46%  rose 0.4%.

In Asia overnight, stocks traded mixed, with markets in China closed in an extension of the Lunar New Year holiday enacted as authorities deal with the effects of the coronavirus outbreak. In Hong Kong, the Hang Seng index HSI, -2.82%  tumbled 2.8%, while Japan’s Nikkei 225 NIK, +0.71%  gained 0.7%.
