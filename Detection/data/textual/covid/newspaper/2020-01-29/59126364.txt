Wednesday evening news briefing: Families taken from Wuhan to Britain may be split up

British nationals brought back from Wuhan will be quarantined in a secure facility for 14 days on their return, on the orders of ministers. Around 200 Britons are waiting to be airlifted from the Chinese province, with hopes of a flight back tomorrow. Government sources said the group will be quarantined potentially at a military base for two weeks, regardless of whether they are showing symptoms or not. But British citizens trapped in the city have been told their families must either stay put or split up if they do not all have British nationality. Nick House, an English teacher from Sussex who has lived in Wuhan for 18 months, said he is "livid" at what he was told by the Foreign and Commonwealth Office. Health Secretary Matt Hancock is chairing a meeting of the Cobra emergency committee this afternoon. And here is a reminder of how to prevent infection and keep safe from the virus.

It comes as British Airways revealed it has suspended all direct flights to China after the Government urged Britons not to travel to the country in response to coronavirus. All flights have been suspended until February 3, with the airline's website showing no direct flights to either Shanghai or Beijing until March.
