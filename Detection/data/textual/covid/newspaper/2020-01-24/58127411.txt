 A coronavirus outbreak that originated in Wuhan, China, has killed 26 people and infected more than 900.The virus has spread to at least eight other countries.Authorities have quarantined the entire city of Wuhan and a handful of other cities, with all transportation halted.Visit Business Insider's homepage for more stories.The death toll of a coronavirus outbreak sweeping China has hit 26, with around 912 people infected worldwide. The virus is thought to have originated at a wet market in the city of Wuhan, and has spread to at least eight other countries: Thailand, Japan, South Korea, Taiwan, Vietnam, Singapore, Nepal, and the US. Two US cases have been reported so far: A man in his 30s in Washington state and a woman in her 60s in Chicago.The scientific name for the virus is 2019-nCoV, and it can pass from human to human. Authorities are rushing to study it and prevent the disease from spreading further as millions begin to travel for the upcoming Chinese New Year celebration. The disease is part of the larger coronavirus family, which typically infect the nose, throat, or sinuses.
