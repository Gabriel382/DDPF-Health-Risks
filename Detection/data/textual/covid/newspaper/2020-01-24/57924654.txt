Death Toll from Wuhan Coronavirus Rises to 25, Over 800 Cases Confirmed 
 The death toll from a new China coronavirus has risen to 25 including a Chinese who was staying far from the disease’s epicenter. 


The Chinese National Health Commission said Friday morning that eight more people died the previous day from the so-called Wuhan pneumonia while 259 more people became also sickened by the illness. 


Most of the victims were living in the central Chinese city of Wuhan, the epicenter of the mysterious respiratory ailment first reported late December. However, a 80-year-old man in northern Chinese province of Hebei near Beijing also died of the infection, becoming the first victim outside Hubei Province. 


Among a total of 830 confirmed patients in mainland China so far, 177 were in serious conditions while only 34 people have been discharged. 


Hong Kong and Macao also confirmed two cases, each, while one person in Taiwan also tested positive for the coronavirus. 


The number of confirmed cases outside Great China Region also rose to nine, including one in Singapore.
