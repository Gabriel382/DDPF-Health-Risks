Chicago woman confirmed as 2nd coronavirus case in U.S. 
 A Chicago woman has become the second U.S. resident diagnosed with China’s newly discovered pneumonia-like virus, federal health authorities confirmed Friday.The patient, who is in her 60s, recently traveled to Wuhan, the Chinese city where the infection is believed to have originated, according to the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention.The announcement comes days after a Washington State man was diagnosed with the illness. The man had also traveled to Wuhan and has been isolated at a Seattle-area hospital.The mysterious coronavirus has killed at least 26 people and sickened more than 800 others in mainland China, forcing the country to shut down public transportation in entire cities just days before its most popular festival, the Lunar New Year holiday.Dr. Nancy Messonnier, director of the CDC’s National Center for Immunization and Respiratory Diseases, warned that the U.S. will likely see more cases in the coming days.“This is a rapidly changing situation,” she told reporters at a news briefing Friday morning.This is a developing story.
