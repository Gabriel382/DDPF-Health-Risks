Centre asks states to be alert to deal with any sudden cases of Chinese coronavirus | India News - Times of India

NEW DELHI: In a precautionary move, the health ministry has asked states to be fully geared up to deal with any sudden case of novel coronavirus that has infected more than 200 people in neighbouring China. Three people have also died.In a letter to chief secretaries, health secretary Preeti Sudan asked states to review preparedness, identify gaps and strengthen core capacities in the area of surveillance, laboratory support, infection prevention, risk communication and hospital preparedness to deal with any sudden case of the respiratory illness.“Any travel related case, reported in the community would be picked up and contacts shall be followed up by Integrated Disease Surveillance Programme… This is an apt opportunity for the states to get reviewed the preparedness, identify gaps and strengthen core capacities in the area of surveillance, laboratory support infection prevention & control, logistics, risk communication and in particular, hospital preparedness in terms of isolation and ventilator management of critically ill patients,” the letter said.Coronavirus is a large family of viruses, which causes illnesses ranging from common cold to more severe diseases such as Middle East Respiratory Syndrome (MERS-CoV) and Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome (SARS-CoV). They also circulate in animals including camels, cats and bats. Rarely, animal coronaviruses can also evolve and infect people, according to the World Health Organisation (WHO).The Centre has already issued a travel advisory for those visiting China and instructed screening of travellers from the neighbouring country at Delhi, Mumbai and Kolkata airports using thermal scanners.Though as per WHO’s risk assessment, the risk of global spread, as of now, remains low with no clear evidence of sustained human to human transmission, cases have also been reported from Japan, Thailand and South Korea.Sudan said the ministry is closely monitoring the situation and as a precautionary move reviewed the core capacities needed to respond to a possible disease importation. National Institute of Virology in Pune has been geared up to test samples of the coronavirus. Besides, ten other laboratories are also equipped to test such samples.
