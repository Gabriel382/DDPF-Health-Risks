PM says travel advice to Wuhan upgraded, as man in Australia tested for new coronavirus 
 “We are well prepared to respond to this situation and we will continue to monitor and take action where necessary,” the Prime Minister said on Twitter.
People travelling to Wuhan are now being advised to exercise a high degree of caution, while the travel advice for the rest of China has not changed.
“If you have travelled to Wuhan and have developed symptoms of respiratory illness, contact your doctor,” the Smart Traveller website said.
