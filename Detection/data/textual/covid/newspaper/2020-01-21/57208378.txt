Wuhan flu sparks profit taking in Hong Kong, weighs on stocks 
 Markets slumped across Asia on Tuesday, with Hong Kong stocks recording their biggest daily loss in more than five months, on deepening fears over the outbreak of a new pneumonia coronavirus in mainland China, as well as a downgrade in Hong Kong’s rating by Moody’s Investors Service.The Hang Seng Index tumbled by 2.8 per cent, or 801.58 points, to close at 27,985.33, falling by the most in a day since August 5.
