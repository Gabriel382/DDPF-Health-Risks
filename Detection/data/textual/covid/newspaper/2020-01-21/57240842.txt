China’s mystery coronavirus: everything you need to know from symptoms to treatment 
 China’s mystery new coronavirus has so far infected at least 291 people and killed six, with the World Health Organization set to meet to determine whether the outbreak should be categorised as an international health emergency.

While the vast bulk of cases have been confined to China, cases in this fast-moving outbreak have now been found in Thailand, South Korea, Japan and the Philippines.

And experts the world over are on high alert after authorities confirmed that the virus could jump from human to human.

But although it remains unlikely that the outbreak will grow into a global epidemic, experts have been warning for years that the world is long overdue a major pandemic.

And so it is sensible to be prepared. This guide will be updated daily and is underpinned with the best advice from leading experts from the NHS and beyond.
