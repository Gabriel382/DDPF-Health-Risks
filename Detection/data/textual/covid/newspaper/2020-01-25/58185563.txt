First case of coronavirus in Australia: update by Chief Medical Officer 
 January 25, 2020. Chief medical officer Professor Brendan Murphy speaks to the media in Canberra about the first reported case of the novel coronavirus in Australia. The man aged in his 50s travelled to Melbourne from Wuhan. Mr Murphy says Australia has a lower risk for the virus due to the Chinese restricting flights out of Wuhan. There are potentially risks from people travelling from Wuhan over the past two weeks. Mr Murphy says there are now over 1300 confirmed cases and the death toll is now 41. (AAP Video/Marc Tewksbury)
